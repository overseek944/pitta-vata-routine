# 04 · Yoga Sequence (Pitta–Vata, ~25–35 min)

**Principles for your constitution:**
- **Slow, steady, smooth-breathed** (Vata calms with grounding; Pitta calms without intensity).
- **Warm up the joints** (Vata stiffness) but **don't overheat** (Pitta).
- Practice in the **cool part of the day** (early morning or evening).
- **End with deep rest** (Shavasana) — grounding is essential for Vata.

**Avoid:** hot/Bikram yoga, fast intense Surya Namaskar, jumpy/jerky transitions, breath-holding that heats, ego/competition, pushing to fatigue.

---

## The sequence (in order)

### 1. Centering — 1 min
Sit comfortably, spine tall, eyes soft. 5 slow breaths.
- **Why / helps:** shifts the nervous system from "doing" to "being." *Pitta–Vata:* settles a racing mind (Vata) and a driven mind (Pitta) before you start.

### 2. Joint warm-ups — Pawanmuktasana series — 3–4 min
Gently rotate: neck, shoulders, wrists, elbows, ankles, knees, hips.
- **Why / helps:** lubricates joints, releases gas/stiffness. *Pitta–Vata:* directly counters Vata's dry, cracking, stiff quality and gets circulation moving without heat.

### 3. Cat–Cow (Marjari-Bitilasana) — 6–8 slow rounds
On hands and knees, inhale arch (cow), exhale round (cat).
- **Why / helps:** mobilizes the spine and **massages abdominal organs → stimulates digestion and bowel movement.** *Pitta–Vata:* gentle, rhythmic, warming-not-heating; great for constipation.

### 4. Chandra Namaskar (Moon Salutation) — 2–3 slow rounds
The cooling, lunar counterpart to Surya Namaskar.
- **Why / helps:** builds gentle warmth and full-body movement **without the heat** of the sun salutation. *Pitta–Vata:* warms Vata, but its "moon" cooling quality keeps Pitta calm. **Use this instead of fast Surya Namaskar.**

### 5. Standing grounding poses — 30–60 sec each
Tadasana (Mountain) → gentle Trikonasana (Triangle) → Vrikshasana (Tree).
- **Why / helps:** builds stability, focus, balance. *Pitta–Vata:* "grounds" scattered Vata energy; held calmly (not pushed) they won't overheat Pitta.

### 6. Forward bends — hold 1–2 min each
Uttanasana (standing) → Paschimottanasana (seated) → Janu Sirsasana (head-to-knee).
- **Why / helps:** deeply **calming and cooling**, quiets the mind, soothes the nervous system, gently compresses the abdomen (aids elimination). *Pitta–Vata:* forward folds are the signature *cooling + grounding* shape — ideal for both.

### 7. Twists — 1 min per side
Seated Ardha Matsyendrasana + a supine twist (lying, knees to one side).
- **Why / helps:** "wring out" the abdominal organs → **stimulate digestion, liver, and bowels; release trapped heat.** *Pitta–Vata:* excellent for digestion/constipation; keep them gentle so they soothe rather than strain.

### 8. Gentle backbend — 30–60 sec
Bhujangasana (Cobra) *or* Setu Bandhasana (Bridge).
- **Why / helps:** opens the front body, stretches the abdomen, stimulates digestion. *Pitta–Vata:* keep it mild — a small opening energizes without the heat of deep/intense backbends.

### 9. Pavanamuktasana (Wind-Relieving Pose) — 1–2 min
Lie on back, hug knees to chest (or one at a time), gentle rocking.
- **Why / helps:** **directly relieves gas, bloating, and constipation** by compressing the colon. *Pitta–Vata:* one of the most useful poses for your exact digestive issue.

### 10. Viparita Karani (Legs up the wall) — 3–5 min
Lie with legs resting up a wall, arms relaxed.
- **Why / helps:** **deeply restorative**, calms the nervous system, relieves fatigue, gentle reversal of circulation. *Pitta–Vata:* cooling + grounding + restful — a perfect "do-nothing" reset for burnout (Pitta) and depletion (Vata).

### 11. Shavasana (Corpse Pose) — 5–8 min
Lie flat, completely still, let the body be heavy, breath natural.
- **Why / helps:** integrates the practice; switches on deep rest. *Pitta–Vata:* **non-negotiable for you** — it grounds Vata and gives over-driven Pitta permission to fully stop. If you skip everything else, don't skip this.

---

## Frequency & pacing
- **Daily** if possible, even a shortened 15-min version (joint warm-ups → cat-cow → 1–2 forward bends → Pavanamuktasana → Shavasana).
- Move **with the breath**, never against it. If you feel hot, sharp, or strained → **slow down or rest.** For you, that sensation is a signal you've tipped into Pitta/Vata aggravation.

---

## 🩸 During menstruation (important adjustment)
While actively bleeding, switch to **gentle / restorative only** — Ayurveda treats the period as a time to rest, and hard practice disturbs the downward flow (Apana Vata).

- ✅ **Do:** Supta Baddha Konasana (reclining bound angle), Child's pose, gentle Cat-Cow, knees-to-chest (Pavanmuktasana), reclining rest.
- ❌ **Avoid while bleeding:** **inversions** (including Legs-up-the-wall / Viparita Karani), **strong twists, deep backbends,** and any **fast/heating** practice.
- Resume the full sequence after the period. Full details: [`10-menstrual-cycle-care.md`](./10-menstrual-cycle-care.md).
