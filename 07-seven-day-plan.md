# 07 · The Detailed 7-Day Plan

This is the heart of the plan. It pairs a **stable daily backbone** (the things that repeat every day) with a **day-by-day variation** so you don't get bored and so each day gently targets a different facet of your Pitta–Vata imbalance.

For **every step** you'll find three things:
- **What** — the action.
- **Why / helps** — the purpose and benefit.
- **Pitta–Vata reason** — why it specifically suits *your* dual imbalance.

> All meals are **dairy-free** (ghee retained). Re-read the north star: **Warm-not-hot · Moist & oily · Regular & calm · Sweet/grounding · Ghee.**

---

## PART A · The Daily Backbone (repeats every day — explained once)

These happen *every* day; the per-day sections below only note variations. The fixed timing is itself the medicine.

| # | What | Why / helps | Pitta–Vata reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Wake ~6:00–6:30, no phone, 5 breaths** | Calm start, no cortisol spike | Gentle waking soothes Vata's jumpiness; unhurried start avoids Pitta "go-mode" |
| 2 | **Tongue scrape + brush** | Clears overnight *ama* (residue), wakes digestion | Removing *ama* supports weak/erratic Vata digestion and clean Pitta agni |
| 3 | **Oil pull (1 tsp coconut oil, optional)** | Oral hygiene, mild detox | Coconut oil is cooling (Pitta) and lubricating (Vata) |
| 4 | **1–2 glasses warm (not hot) water** ± fennel/raisin | Rehydrates colon, triggers gastrocolic reflex → bowel urge | Warm = good for Vata; not-hot = won't aggravate Pitta; core constipation tool |
| 5 | **Same-time toilet sit + footstool, no strain** | Trains the colon to a rhythm | Builds the regularity Vata lacks; no straining avoids heat/irritation (Pitta) |
| 6 | **Aloe vera juice 15–20 ml in warm water** | Cooling + mild laxative | Cools Pitta, gently moves Vata constipation |
| 7 | **Abhyanga (warm-oil self-massage) 5–10 min** | Grounds nervous system, lubricates, clockwise belly aids bowels | THE Pitta–Vata practice: oil counters Vata dryness, cooling oil calms Pitta |
| 8 | **Warm (not hot) shower** | Refresh, keep some oil on skin | Warm comforts Vata; avoiding very hot protects Pitta |
| 9 | **Yoga (see daily emphasis)** | Movement, digestion, calm | Slow + grounding for Vata, cooling + non-intense for Pitta |
| 10 | **Pranayama:** Nadi Shodhana 6–9 · Bhramari 5–7 · Sheetali 3–5 | Balances nervous system | Nadi Shodhana balances both; Bhramari calms; modest Sheetali cools Pitta |
| 11 | **Meditation 10–15 min** | Quiets mind, lowers stress | Settles Vata anxiety + Pitta drive/burnout |
| 12 | **Breakfast (warm, dairy-free)** | Steady morning fuel | Warm/moist for Vata, non-sour/non-spicy for Pitta |
| 13 | **Lunch = biggest meal (~12:30–1:30)** | Eat heaviest when agni peaks | Midday fire digests cleanly → no fermentation (Vata gas) or acidity (Pitta) |
| 14 | **Post-lunch: Vajrasana 3–5 min + short walk** | Aids digestion, prevents heaviness | Movement helps Vata motility; gentle, not heating, for Pitta |
| 15 | **Hydrate: 2.5–3 L warm/room-temp water, CCF tea** | Prevent dryness, support stool | Anti-Vata-dryness; warm-not-iced protects both |
| 16 | **Optional snack (~4–5pm): soaked dates/fruit** | Prevents hunger crash | Stops Pitta "hunger-anger"; sweet+moist calms Vata |
| 17 | **Dinner light & early (~6:30–7:30), kichari-style** | Easy digestion before sleep | Light = no overnight fermentation (Vata); non-spicy = calm Pitta |
| 18 | **Wind-down: screens off ~9pm, dim lights** | Signals sleep, lowers stimulation | Over-stimulation aggravates both; darkness calms |
| 19 | **Bedtime: warm water + 1 tsp ghee + pinch nutmeg + Triphala** | Sleep + overnight bowel support | Ghee lubricates (constipation), nutmeg aids sleep, Triphala regulates — all dairy-free |
| 20 | **Foot/scalp oil massage + 3–5 Bhramari, sleep by 10:30** | Deep calm, good sleep | Foot massage profoundly grounds Vata; sleep prevents both doshas worsening |

> The 7 days below assume this backbone is running. Each day adds a **theme, a food menu, a movement emphasis, and one focus practice.**

## PART B · Day by Day

### 🌱 DAY 1 — "Rhythm & Rehydration"
**Intention:** establish the timing skeleton and flood the dry system with warm moisture.

**Food**
- **Breakfast:** Cooked oats in water + 1 tsp ghee + 4 soaked raisins + cardamom.
  - *Why/helps:* warm, soft, sweet start; raisins add gentle fiber+moisture.
  - *Pitta–Vata:* moist & sweet calms both; no sour/spice.
- **Lunch:** Basmati rice + mung dal + ghee-sautéed lauki & carrot + 1 tsp ghee.
  - *Why/helps:* complete, easy-to-digest midday meal.
  - *Pitta–Vata:* mung + rice + ghee = the most balancing trio for both.
- **Snack:** 3 soaked dates.
- **Dinner:** Simple kichari (see recipe in [`03-food-guide.md`](./03-food-guide.md)).
  - *Why/helps:* lightest, most healing dinner; preps the gut overnight.
  - *Pitta–Vata:* warm/moist/oily/sweet — textbook for the combo.

**Movement emphasis:** full yoga sequence, with **extra Cat-Cow + Pavanamuktasana** (digestion focus).
- *Why/helps:* kick-starts sluggish bowels on day one.
- *Pitta–Vata:* gentle abdominal massage moves Vata-constipation without heat.

**Focus practice:** simply **hit every timing**. Don't worry about perfection.
- *Pitta–Vata reason:* regularity is the #1 medicine for Vata, and not-skipping prevents Pitta flares. Today is about the clock, not intensity.

---

### 🔥 DAY 2 — "Cool the Fire"
**Intention:** lower Pitta heat (acidity, irritability).

**Food**
- **Breakfast:** Stewed pear + cinnamon + 4 soaked, peeled almonds.
  - *Pitta–Vata:* pear & cinnamon are cooling-sweet (Pitta), soaked almonds add grounding oil (Vata).
- **Lunch:** Rice + mung dal + ghee-cooked zucchini & pumpkin + coriander garnish + **CCF tea**.
  - *Pitta–Vata:* coriander & fennel are cooling digestives — calm Pitta acidity while aiding Vata digestion.
- **Snack:** ½ tsp **Gulkand** + a few soaked raisins.
  - *Why/helps:* rose cools and soothes the gut lining.
  - *Pitta–Vata:* a targeted Pitta cooler that's gentle on Vata.
- **Dinner:** Kichari with extra ghee + a little fresh coriander.

**Movement emphasis:** longer **forward bends + Chandra Namaskar**; keep everything cool and slow.
- *Pitta–Vata:* forward folds and the "moon" salutation actively reduce Pitta heat while grounding Vata.

**Focus practice:** **Sheetali (cooling breath) 5 rounds, twice today** (morning + late afternoon).
- *Why/helps:* directly cools body and mind.
- *Pitta–Vata reason:* targets the Pitta side of your imbalance; kept modest so it doesn't chill Vata.

---

### 💨 DAY 3 — "Ground the Wind"
**Intention:** settle Vata (anxiety, dryness, irregularity, gas).

**Food**
- **Breakfast:** Warm oat porridge with **almond milk** + ghee + soaked figs.
  - *Pitta–Vata:* extra oil + moisture deeply anti-Vata; figs help the bowels; dairy-free.
- **Lunch:** Rice + mung dal + ghee-cooked sweet potato & green beans + 1–2 tsp ghee.
  - *Pitta–Vata:* sweet, heavy-ish, oily root veg grounds Vata; cooked soft so it's easy.
- **Snack:** Soaked dates + 2 soaked walnuts/almonds.
- **Dinner:** Creamy kichari (a bit more water + ghee) — extra unctuous.
  - *Pitta–Vata:* the moister, oilier version is calming for a dry, anxious Vata evening.

**Movement emphasis:** **grounding standing poses + long Shavasana (10 min) + Legs-up-the-wall (5 min)**.
- *Pitta–Vata:* stability poses and deep rest are the antidote to Vata's mobile, scattered, depleting nature.

**Focus practice:** **Bhramari 7 rounds** morning and night + extra-long **foot massage** before bed.
- *Why/helps:* vagus-nerve calm + grounding through the feet.
- *Pitta–Vata reason:* directly soothes Vata anxiety and supports the deep sleep Vata often lacks.

---

### ⚖️ DAY 4 — "Balance & Digestion"
**Intention:** strengthen digestion (agni) gently — the root of both doshas' gut symptoms.

**Food**
- **Breakfast:** Moong dal cheela (thin savory pancake) with cumin + coriander.
  - *Pitta–Vata:* protein-light, warm, mildly spiced — kindles agni without heat.
- **Lunch:** Rice + mixed vegetable + mung dal + **small piece of fresh ginger** cooked in + ghee.
  - *Why/helps:* a little ginger sparks digestion.
  - *Pitta–Vata:* small/cooked ginger aids Vata digestion; kept small so Pitta isn't overheated.
- **Snack:** CCF tea + a date.
- **Dinner:** Kichari + a few drops lime *only if* no acidity (skip if Pitta is high).

**Movement emphasis:** **twists + Cat-Cow + Pavanamuktasana** (digestive set).
- *Pitta–Vata:* twists "wring" the organs to boost digestion and elimination — good for both gas (Vata) and sluggish bowels.

**Focus practice:** mindful eating — **eat every meal slowly, no screens, fully chew.**
- *Why/helps:* digestion starts in the mouth and calm state.
- *Pitta–Vata reason:* hurried/distracted eating aggravates Vata (irregular) and Pitta (eating "hot/fast"); slowing down improves both.

---

### 🧘 DAY 5 — "Calm the Mind"
**Intention:** address the emotional core — Pitta frustration/burnout + Vata anxiety/overwhelm.

**Food** (same balancing pattern)
- **Breakfast:** Stewed apple + cinnamon + cardamom + ghee.
- **Lunch:** Rice + mung dal + ghee-cooked spinach & carrot + coriander.
  - *Pitta–Vata:* cooked greens (bitter, cooling) calm Pitta; ghee keeps it Vata-friendly.
- **Snack:** Gulkand + soaked raisins.
- **Dinner:** Kichari.

**Movement emphasis:** **gentle, restorative-only practice** — Child's pose, supported forward bends, Legs-up-the-wall, long Shavasana.
- *Pitta–Vata reason:* on a "mind" day, doing *less* is the point — restorative yoga lowers both the Pitta drive and Vata restlessness.

**Focus practice:** **20-minute meditation** (or yoga nidra) + a **technology-light evening**.
- *Why/helps:* deep nervous-system reset.
- *Pitta–Vata reason:* your strongest symptoms are mental; this directly treats burnout (Pitta) and overwhelm (Vata). Also notice and release the urge to "do it perfectly" — that urge is Pitta itself.

---

### 🌿 DAY 6 — "Nourish & Rebuild"
**Intention:** rebuild strength/ojas — Vata–Pitta imbalance often leaves you depleted.

**Food**
- **Breakfast:** Oat porridge (almond milk) + ghee + soaked figs + **½ tsp Shatavari stirred in**.
  - *Why/helps:* Shatavari nourishes and rejuvenates.
  - *Pitta–Vata:* sweet + cooling + grounding — restorative for both.
- **Lunch:** Rice + mung dal + ghee-cooked mixed veg + 2 tsp ghee + soaked almonds on the side.
  - *Pitta–Vata:* slightly richer to rebuild tissue; still cooling and easy.
- **Snack:** Dates + soaked almonds.
- **Dinner:** Kichari + a little extra ghee.

**Movement emphasis:** **gentle full sequence, no strain**, ending with Viparita Karani + Shavasana.
- *Pitta–Vata:* nourishing days pair light movement with deep rest, not exertion (which depletes Vata and heats Pitta).

**Focus practice:** **early to bed (by 10:00)** + full bedtime ritual.
- *Why/helps:* sleep is when the body rebuilds.
- *Pitta–Vata reason:* both doshas heal with rest; extra sleep directly counters depletion and burnout.

---

### 🔄 DAY 7 — "Integrate & Reflect"
**Intention:** consolidate the week, notice what helped, plan the simplest sustainable version.

**Food** (your favorites from the week)
- **Breakfast:** whichever breakfast you felt best after.
- **Lunch:** your favorite balanced plate.
- **Snack:** soaked fruit / CCF tea.
- **Dinner:** kichari (a calm, restful close to the week).

**Movement emphasis:** your favorite combination from the week, kept gentle.

**Focus practice:** **a 10-minute written reflection** (see prompts below) + plan which habits to keep.
- *Why/helps:* turns a 7-day experiment into a lasting lifestyle.
- *Pitta–Vata reason:* reflection harnesses Pitta's intelligence *constructively* (instead of self-criticism), and committing to a stable simple routine gives Vata the regularity it craves.

**Reflection prompts:**
1. Which days did I have a bowel movement? What seemed to help most?
2. When did I feel calmest? Most irritable? Most anxious? What preceded each?
3. Which 3–5 habits felt easy *and* helpful? (Keep these.)
4. Which felt like a burden? (Drop or simplify them.)
5. What is my realistic "minimum viable routine" for next week?

## PART C · After Day 7

- **Don't restart from zero.** Keep the daily backbone running and repeat the day-themes loosely (e.g., a "cool the fire" focus when Pitta is up, a "ground the wind" focus when anxious/constipated/travelling).
- **Adjust to season & state:** more cooling in summer/heat (Pitta), more warm/oily/grounding in cold, windy, or stressful/travel times (Vata).
- **Re-read [`09-rollout-and-habit-tracker.md`](./09-rollout-and-habit-tracker.md)** to lock habits in without overwhelm.

> The success metric is **not** a perfect week. It's that 3–5 of these habits quietly become "just how you live." For your constitution, *gentle and consistent always beats intense and complete.*
