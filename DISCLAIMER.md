# ⚠️ Disclaimer

**This repository is a personal wellness reference, not medical advice.**

- The content is based on **traditional Ayurvedic principles** and general wellness guidance. It has **not** been reviewed by a physician and is **not** a diagnosis, prescription, or treatment plan.
- **Individual response varies.** Concepts like "prakruti" (constitution) and "dosha balance" are traditional frameworks; they are not a substitute for modern medical evaluation.
- **Before acting on this plan**, especially if you:
  - take any prescription medication,
  - are pregnant or breastfeeding,
  - have a diagnosed condition (heart, liver, kidney, gallbladder, GI disease, diabetes, high cholesterol, etc.),
  - or have allergies,
  please consult a **qualified physician** and/or a **registered Ayurvedic practitioner (vaidya).**

## Herbs & remedies
- Introduce **one herb at a time**, at a **low dose**, and observe how you respond.
- Herbs can **interact with medications** — clear them with a professional first.
- **Aloe vera** (latex/aloin), **senna**, and **castor oil** are laxatives; misuse can cause cramping, diarrhoea, dehydration, and electrolyte imbalance. Use only as described, and not in pregnancy.
- Stop any item that causes adverse effects.

## When to see a doctor (don't self-treat)
Seek medical care promptly if you have:
- Constipation lasting **more than 1–2 weeks**, or a sudden change in bowel habits
- **Blood** in stool, **black** stools, or unexplained **weight loss**
- **Severe or persistent abdominal pain**, vomiting, or fever
- Inability to pass stool **or gas** (possible obstruction — urgent)
- Any symptom that is severe, worsening, or worrying

## Food & allergies
- This plan is **dairy-free** by request (ghee retained). If you are allergic to **nuts** (almond/oat milk, almonds), **sesame** (oil), or any ingredient listed, substitute or omit it.
