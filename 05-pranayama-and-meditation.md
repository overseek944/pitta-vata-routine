# 05 · Pranayama & Meditation

Breathwork is the fastest, most direct lever on the nervous system — and for a Pitta–Vata mind (driven *and* anxious), it's medicine. Practice **after asana, before meditation,** seated comfortably with a tall spine.

> General rule for you: **favor slow, smooth, balancing, cooling breaths; avoid forceful heating ones.**

---

## ✅ Your core practices

### 1. Nadi Shodhana — Alternate-Nostril Breathing ⭐ (6–9 rounds)
**How:**
1. Right hand: fold index + middle finger (or use thumb and ring finger).
2. Close **right** nostril with thumb → inhale slowly through **left**.
3. Close **left** with ring finger → release thumb → exhale slowly through **right**.
4. Inhale **right** → close right → exhale **left**. *That's one round.*
5. Keep the breath slow, smooth, equal. No strain, no breath-holding at first.

- **Why / helps:** balances the left/right (lunar/solar) channels and the autonomic nervous system. **The single best pranayama for a dual Pitta–Vata imbalance** — it calms *and* grounds *and* cools, all at once. Reduces anxiety (Vata) and irritability (Pitta).

### 2. Bhramari — Humming-Bee Breath (5–7 rounds)
**How:** inhale normally; on a long exhale, make a soft, low **"hmmmm"** humming sound (lips closed). Optionally close ears with thumbs, rest fingers on face.
- **Why / helps:** the vibration soothes the **vagus nerve** → quick calm, less mental chatter. *Pitta–Vata:* eases both **Vata anxiety/overwhelm** and **Pitta frustration/anger**. Excellent any time you feel wound up, and again at bedtime.

### 3. Sheetali / Sheetkari — Cooling Breath (3–5 rounds, modest)
**How (Sheetali):** curl the tongue into a tube, inhale slowly through it, close mouth, exhale through the nose.
**How (Sheetkari):** if you can't curl your tongue, gently clench teeth, inhale through them, exhale through nose.
- **Why / helps:** literally cools the body and mind — direct antidote to high Pitta (heat, acidity, anger).
- **⚠️ Why only modest amounts for you:** cooling breath, overdone, can aggravate **Vata** (cold/dry). Keep it to a few rounds, and skip on cold days or when you feel anxious/chilly.

---

## ❌ Minimize / avoid (too heating for you)
- **Bhastrika** (bellows breath) — strongly heating.
- **Long, fast Kapalabhati** (skull-shining) — heating and, done fast, can scatter Vata.
- A few gentle rounds occasionally are fine, but they should **not** be your main practice.

---

## Suggested daily order (≈8–10 min)
1. Nadi Shodhana — 6–9 rounds
2. Bhramari — 5–7 rounds
3. Sheetali/Sheetkari — 3–5 rounds
→ then sit straight into meditation.

---

## 🧘 Meditation (10–15 min)

Pick **one** and keep it consistent (consistency > technique for Vata):

- **Breath awareness:** rest attention on the natural breath; when the mind wanders, gently return. Grounding for Vata.
- **So-Hum:** silently "So" on the inhale, "Hum" on the exhale. A simple anchor for a busy mind.
- **Guided body-scan / yoga nidra:** especially good if you're depleted or can't settle (deep Vata rest).
- **Loving-kindness / gratitude:** softens Pitta's criticism, impatience, and self-pressure.

**Tips for your constitution:**
- **Same time, same spot** daily — predictability calms Vata.
- Don't grade yourself or "try hard" — that's Pitta turning meditation into another task. The goal is *less effort*, not more.
- Even **10 minutes daily** changes the tone of your whole day. Frequency beats duration.

---

## When you feel a flare (use anytime)
- **Pitta flare** (hot, angry, acidic, impatient): 5–6 rounds **Sheetali** + slow exhales.
- **Vata flare** (anxious, scattered, can't sleep, restless): 6–8 rounds **Bhramari** + 5 min legs-up-the-wall.
- **Either:** a few minutes of **Nadi Shodhana** resets both.
