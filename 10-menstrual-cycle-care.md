# 10 · Menstrual & Cycle-Aware Care (Pitta–Vata)

For a 26-year-old woman following this plan, with **painful periods (cramps), lower-abdomen pain, looser/more frequent stools just before the period, and some faintness.** This file explains why that happens, what to do in the moment, how to adjust the routine on period days, and how to reduce pain over the coming cycles — plus clear medical red flags.

> ⚠️ Painful periods are common, but "much more than other girls" + **faintness** deserve a doctor's check. See the red-flags section and [`DISCLAIMER.md`](./DISCLAIMER.md).

---

## 1. Why this happens (and why it's mostly normal)

- Just before a period, the body releases **prostaglandins**. These make the uterus contract (**cramps**) **and** act on the nearby bowel → **looser, more frequent stools** ("period poops") and lower-belly aching. The cramps and the extra toilet trips are the **same process**, not two separate problems.
- In Ayurveda, menstruation is governed by **Apana Vata** — the *downward-moving* energy that also controls elimination. Cramps = **aggravated Apana Vata**; heat, heavier flow, loose stools, and irritability add the **Pitta** layer. So period pain is very much a **Pitta–Vata** event — which is why the warm, oily, grounding routine already helps.

---

## 2. Acute relief — what to do in the 1–2 days before & during

1. **Heat on the lower belly & back** ⭐ — hot water bottle / heating pad, 15–20 min. The single most effective cramp reliever (relaxes the uterine muscle).
2. **Rest & downshift** — Ayurveda treats menstruation as a time to **rest**. **Stop intense yoga**; do gentle/restorative only (see §4).
3. **Warm, Pitta-safe antispasmodic teas** — **fennel (variyari) tea**, **CCF tea** (cumin+coriander+fennel). *(Prefer fennel over ginger — see the ginger note in [`06`](./06-herbs-remedies-and-constipation.md).)*
4. **Eat enough & stay warm-hydrated** — don't under-eat around the period; warm cooked food, warm water. Under-eating worsens faintness.
5. **Gentle warm-oil rub** (sesame) on the lower back/belly before bed. Skip full-body abhyanga during heavy flow.
6. **Magnesium-rich foods** — soaked nuts/seeds, banana, cooked greens — ease cramps.

---

## 3. Period-day tweaks to the routine

She's stacked several **mild laxatives** (lemon water + papaya + ghee, ± aloe). Great on normal days — but **the period itself already loosens the bowels**, so during this window they add to the 3–4 toilet trips.

- **Ease off the laxative elements during the period:**
  - **Pause the lemon** in the warm water (also: sour → aggravates Pitta & loose stools) → use plain warm water or **fennel water**.
  - **Pause aloe vera** if she's taking it.
- **Swap the daily papaya for a cooling fruit** during the period. Papaya is **heating and mildly uterus-stimulating** — fine for constipation on normal days, but around the period use **pear, cooked apple, soaked raisins/figs, or pomegranate** instead.
- **Add all of these back after the period ends.**
- **Keep everything else** (6:30 wake, warm water, mung dal, ghee at night, 10:30 sleep) — it's working.

---

## 4. Yoga during menstruation

Do **gentle / restorative only** while bleeding:
- ✅ Supta Baddha Konasana (reclining bound angle), Child's pose, gentle Cat-Cow, knees-to-chest (Pavanmuktasana), reclining rest.
- ❌ **Avoid inversions** (including legs-up-the-wall/Viparita Karani), **strong twists, deep backbends,** and any **fast/heating** practice while bleeding.
- Resume the full sequence after the period.

---

## 5. Reducing pain over the next cycles (builds over 2–3 months)

- **Keep the Vata-pacifying routine** — warm, oily, ghee, regular meals, good sleep, low stress (cramps worsen with stress and irregularity).
- **The week before the period:** extra warm/cooked food; avoid cold, raw, spicy, sour, caffeine.
- **Cycle-friendly foods:** **fennel (variyari)**, **sesame seeds + a little jaggery (til-gud)**, **magnesium foods**, cooked greens.
- **Shatavari** ⭐ — the classic women's tonic; sweet, cooling, grounding — fits her Pitta–Vata and supports hormonal/reproductive balance. Full guide: [`12-shatavari-guide.md`](./12-shatavari-guide.md).
- **Iron** — the faintness is a flag; build iron gently (without worsening constipation): [`11-iron-and-anemia.md`](./11-iron-and-anemia.md).

---

## 6. 🚩 Red flags — see a gynaecologist

Painful periods are common, but **book a doctor** if:
- Pain is **severe enough to disrupt her day**, is **getting worse over cycles**, or isn't relieved by heat/rest → rule out **endometriosis, PCOS, ovarian cysts, fibroids** (all treatable).
- **Faintness/weakness is recurrent** → check **hemoglobin + ferritin** (possible anaemia). Also check **B12 & folate** (common in vegetarians).
- **Very heavy bleeding, large clots, periods > 7 days, or pain outside period days.**

**Safe pain relief:** for most young women, an **NSAID (ibuprofen or mefenamic acid) taken at the first sign of cramps** is very effective — but take it **with food** and **on a doctor's/pharmacist's advice** (given her sensitive stomach), not on her own.

> Reassurance: period pain varies a lot between women — she isn't "abnormal." But she doesn't have to just endure it; heat, rest, fennel, the routine, Shatavari + iron, and a one-time doctor check give real options.
