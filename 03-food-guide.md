# 03 · Food Guide (Dairy-Free, Pitta–Vata)

Food is the most powerful lever you have. The rule: **favor sweet, warm, moist, well-cooked, lightly-spiced, oily** food; **reduce pungent (spicy), sour, salty, fried, raw, and cold.**

> Dairy note: **no milk** in this plan. **Ghee is kept** (clarified — virtually lactose/casein-free) because it is the single best food for Pitta–Vata + constipation. Where a creamy element helps, use **almond or oat milk**.

---

## The six tastes, for your doshas

| Taste | Effect on Vata | Effect on Pitta | Verdict for you |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sweet** (grains, ghee, sweet fruit, dates) | ↓ calms | ↓ calms | ⭐ **Favor most** |
| Astringent (lentils, many veg) | ↑ mild | ↓ calms | OK in cooked form |
| Bitter (leafy greens, turmeric) | ↑ mild | ↓ calms | OK in moderation |
| Sour (citrus, vinegar, fermented) | ↓ calms | ↑ **aggravates** | ⚠️ Minimal |
| Salty | ↓ calms | ↑ **aggravates** | ⚠️ Moderate |
| **Pungent** (chili, raw onion/garlic) | ↑ aggravates | ↑ **aggravates** | ❌ **Avoid most** |

**Takeaway:** lean on **sweet**, keep food **warm and oily**, and cut **spicy** hardest.

## How to build each meal

### 🍳 Breakfast (warm, light-moderate)
- Cooked oats/porridge (water or almond milk) + ghee + soaked raisins/dates + cardamom
- Stewed apple/pear + cinnamon + soaked peeled almonds
- Moong dal cheela or mild poha
- Warm fennel/CCF tea

### 🍛 Lunch (the biggest meal — midday)
Assemble:
1. **Grain:** rice (basmati) or soft chapati
2. **Protein:** mung dal / lentils (best) — or paneer if you ever reintroduce dairy
3. **Cooked vegetables** sautéed in ghee with cumin/coriander/turmeric
4. **1–2 tsp ghee** on top (don't skip)
5. Optional: warm CCF tea to sip
- *Why biggest at midday:* your digestive fire (agni) peaks 10 am–2 pm — heavy food digests cleanly now and won't sit and ferment.

### 🥣 Dinner (light & early)
- **Kichari** is the gold standard (recipe below).
- Or: vegetable soup + soft rice, dal-rice, cooked veg + chapati.
- Finish 2.5–3 hrs before bed.

## ⭐ Core recipe: Healing Kichari (dairy-free, 1–2 servings)

The most Pitta–Vata-balancing, constipation-friendly, easy-to-digest meal in Ayurveda.

**Ingredients**
- ½ cup basmati rice
- ¼ cup split yellow mung dal (soak both 20–30 min, rinse)
- 1–2 tbsp **ghee**
- ½ tsp cumin seeds
- ½ tsp coriander powder
- ¼ tsp turmeric
- ½ tsp fennel seeds
- 1-inch piece fresh ginger (small, optional), grated
- 1–1.5 cups chopped soft vegetables (lauki, carrot, zucchini, pumpkin)
- 3.5–4 cups water
- Rock salt to taste (modest), fresh coriander leaves to garnish

**Method**
1. Heat ghee on low; add cumin + fennel until fragrant (don't brown — keep it gentle for Pitta).
2. Add ginger, turmeric, coriander; stir 10 sec.
3. Add rice, dal, vegetables; stir to coat.
4. Add water, bring to a boil, then simmer **covered 25–30 min** until soft and porridge-like.
5. Add a little salt, garnish with coriander, finish with a small extra spoon of ghee.

*Why it works:* warm + moist + oily (ghee) + sweet (rice) + easy protein (mung) = soothes Vata dryness, doesn't overheat Pitta, lubricates the bowel. Mild spices aid digestion without aggravating Pitta.

## Eating habits (as important as the food)
- **Never skip meals** (Vata hates irregularity; Pitta turns hunger into acid/anger).
- **Eat at the same times daily.**
- **Eat warm, freshly cooked** food; minimize cold leftovers and raw.
- **Sit, unhurried, no screens;** don't eat when angry or anxious (you digest your mood too).
- **Don't overeat** — leave ~¼ of the stomach empty.
- **Sip warm water** with meals, not large amounts of cold water.

## Dates / Kharek

Dates (*kharjura*) are **sweet, cooling, moist, and grounding** — one of the *few* fruits that calm **both** Pitta and Vata, and they gently help constipation. Great fit.

- ✅ **Best:** soft ripe dates, or **fresh kharek soaked** (2–4 overnight) — soaking softens them, cuts astringency, and turns them into a gentle laxative.
- ⚠️ **Firm/crunchy fresh kharek** is more **astringent/tannic** → slightly drying (Vata) and heavier to digest; in excess it can even tighten the bowels. Go easy on the very crunchy ones.
- **Amount:** **2–4 a day**, chewed well. They're **heavy (guru)** — don't overeat, and skip on heavy/bloated/low-appetite days.
- **Pair with a little ghee** for extra digestibility and bowel benefit.

## Paneer vs Mung dal (protein) — why mung wins for her

Paneer is **dairy (milk being avoided), heavy, and tends to be constipating** — so it works against the goals. **Mung dal is the better protein:** light, easy to digest, non-binding, dairy-free.

**Protein (approx.):**
| Food | Protein |
|---|---|
| Mung dal — **dry/uncooked** | **~24 g / 100 g** |
| Mung dal — **cooked** (as dal) | ~7–9 g / 100 g (soaks up water) |
| Paneer — full-fat | ~18–20 g / 100 g (+ fat, dairy) |

**Equivalence:** **100 g paneer (~18 g protein) ≈ 75 g dry mung dal ≈ ~1 cup / 1 katori cooked dal.**
Rule of thumb: **3 parts dry mung dal = 4 parts paneer** in protein.

- Think in **dry weight** — a bowl of watery dal is protein-dilute.
- **Pair dal with rice** → a *complete* protein (kichari already does this ✅).
- If eating paneer occasionally: fresh, small, **at lunch**, lightly cooked with cumin/coriander — never cold, fried, or at night.

### How to eat mung dal properly
- ⚠️ **Always COOK it — don't drink it raw-blended.** Raw/only-soaked mung (even blended into a drink) is **gas-forming and hard to digest** → aggravates Vata.
- **Soak → cook soft →** (blend smooth *after cooking* if you want a "liquid"/soup form).
- ⚠️ **Don't strain and drink only the water** — the **protein is in the solids**. Blend and keep the whole cooked dal.
- Add **ghee + cumin + coriander + fennel + a pinch of hing** (anti-gas) + optional *small* cooked ginger. No chili.
- **Amount:** ¼–½ cup **dry** dal per meal; eat **warm**, ideally at lunch, **with rice**.
- **Best forms:** kichari ⭐ > soft cooked dal > smooth cooked-then-blended soup > lightly-cooked sprouts.

## Almond milk (dairy-free) — what it is & how to make it

Almond milk is **not dairy and not milk+almond** — it's simply a **water extract of blended soaked almonds** (almonds + water, strained). 100% milk-free, which is why it's the go-to here.

**Homemade (purest):**
1. **Soak** raw almonds overnight (8–12 hrs).
2. **Peel** them (removes the heating/bitter skin → gentler on Pitta).
3. **Blend** with fresh water — ~**1 cup almonds : 3–4 cups water**.
4. **Strain** through a cloth/nut-milk bag → the liquid **is** the almond milk (leftover pulp = almond meal for cooking).
5. Optional: pinch of **cardamom** or a soaked date. Warm gently before use.

**If buying:** choose **unsweetened**, short ingredient list (avoid added sugar, thickeners).

**When to use it:** as the (optional) carrier for Shatavari, in morning porridge, or **warm at bedtime + ghee + nutmeg** (sleep drink). Don't drink a big glass *with* a salty/sour main meal.
