# 11 · Boosting Iron (without worsening constipation, Pitta, or Vata)

The faintness around her period is a flag for possible **low iron / anaemia** (very common in young women). The elegant part: **the best iron foods for her are also the ones that relieve constipation and calm both doshas** — so you can hit all three at once.

> ⚠️ **Test before you supplement.** Get **hemoglobin + ferritin** checked (plus **B12 + folate** for vegetarians) before taking iron pills — too much iron is harmful, and the faintness may not even be iron. See [`DISCLAIMER.md`](./DISCLAIMER.md).

---

## The golden rule
**Skip iron tablets as the first move.** Standard iron pills (ferrous sulfate) commonly cause **constipation and acidity** — the opposite of her goals. Go **food-first**, choosing iron foods that also keep the bowels moving.

---

## ⭐ Best iron foods for HER (iron + constipation-relief + dosha-friendly)

| Food | Why it's perfect for her |
|---|---|
| **Soaked dates, black raisins (munakka), figs, prunes** | Iron-rich **and** natural laxatives; sweet-cooling-grounding = calm both doshas. *Top pick.* |
| **Amla (Indian gooseberry / આમળા)** ⭐ | High **vitamin C** (boosts iron absorption) + **cooling** (Pitta) + aids digestion; already in her Triphala |
| **Pomegranate (દાડમ)** | Blood-building, **cooling**, juicy — very Pitta-friendly |
| **Beetroot (cooked)** | Supports hemoglobin; easy on Vata when cooked |
| **Cooked leafy greens** — spinach, methi, amaranth/rajgira | Iron + folate; **cook them** (raw = gas for Vata) |
| **Black sesame (kala til)** | Very iron-rich, grounding (Vata) — use in **moderation** (heating for Pitta) |
| **Ragi (finger millet / નાગલી)** | One of the highest plant iron+calcium grains; cook soft with ghee |
| **Lentils** — mung, masoor (red) | Iron + protein; already eating mung ✅ |
| **Soaked pumpkin & sunflower seeds** | Iron + magnesium (also eases period cramps) |
| **Jaggery (ગોળ), small amount** | Traditional iron/blood builder — warming, so keep small; classic as **til-gud** |

---

## Absorption boosters (do these)
- **Pair iron with vitamin C in the same meal** — amla, pomegranate, guava, a little lemon (on non-period days). Can multiply absorption.
- **Cook in a cast-iron pan (loha kadai)** — a genuine, traditional way to add iron to food.
- **Soak / sprout / cook** dals & grains — reduces the phytates that block iron.

## Absorption blockers (avoid *with* iron meals)
- **Tea & coffee** (tannins) — don't drink within ~1 hr of iron-rich meals (she avoids coffee anyway).
- **Calcium at the same time** — not an issue for her (dairy-free; ghee is fine).

---

## If a supplement is needed (only after testing)
1. Confirm low iron with a **blood test** first.
2. Ask the doctor for a **gentler form** — e.g. **ferrous bisglycinate (chelated iron)** or iron polymaltose — far **less constipating** than ferrous sulfate.
3. **Ayurvedic iron tonics** (gentler on the gut) exist — e.g. *Punarnava Mandur, Dhatri Lauha, Lohasava, Drakshasava* — but take **only under a vaidya's guidance.**
4. Also address **B12 & folate** if low.

---

## Simple daily iron plan (ties into her routine)
- **Morning:** soaked dates + black raisins + a fig; a vitamin-C source (amla or pomegranate).
- **Lunch:** cooked greens and/or beetroot with the mung dal; cook in a **cast-iron pan**.
- **Snack:** soaked pumpkin/sunflower seeds.
- **Avoid:** tea/coffee with meals.
- **Test:** hemoglobin, ferritin, B12, folate before any iron pills.
